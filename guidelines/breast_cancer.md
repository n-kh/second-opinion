# NCCN Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology: Breast Cancer (v2.2026)

## 1. Staging Workup
- Diagnostic mammogram, breast ultrasound, and pathological confirmation.
- Biomarker status must be evaluated: Estrogen Receptor (ER), Progesterone Receptor (PR), and HER2 status.
- Genetic testing is recommended for patients diagnosed under age 50, triple-negative breast cancer, or strong family history.

## 2. HER2-Positive Early/Locally Advanced Breast Cancer (Stage II-III)
- Standard of Care: Systemic therapy containing chemotherapy plus HER2-targeted agents (Trastuzumab + Pertuzumab) is indicated.
- Neoadjuvant (pre-operative) therapy is preferred for tumors >= 2cm or node-positive disease to downstage the tumor and assess pathological response.
- Adjuvant therapy: Complete surgical resection followed by HER2-targeted therapy to complete 1 year. Radiation therapy is indicated post-lumpectomy or post-mastectomy with positive nodes.
- Endocrine therapy is indicated if ER/PR is positive.
